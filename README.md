# Descriptive Statistical Analysis of Audience Reviews: A Case Study of *3 Idiots*


The objective of this project is to analyze audience reviews of the movie 3 Idiots collected from IMDb using **descriptive statistical techniques**. By combining statistical calculations with graphical visualizations, the project aims to provide meaningful insights into the movie's popularity and overall audience reception.

---

## 📂 Dataset

**Source:** IMDb Audience Reviews

| Attribute | Value |
|------------|------------|
| Original Reviews Collected | 1,086 |
| Clean Reviews Used | 1,042 |
| Retention Rate | 96% |
| Rating Scale | 1–10 |

### Dataset Features

| Feature | Description |
|----------|------------|
| Username | Reviewer identifier |
| Rating | Numerical audience rating |
| Review Title | Short review summary |
| Review Text | Full audience review |

## 📌 Major Insights

### ⭐ Exceptional Audience Reception

The mean (8.67), median (9), and mode (10) all lie near the maximum rating, indicating consistently strong audience appreciation.

### 📊 Strong Consensus Among Viewers

Almost 89% of all ratings fall within the 7–10 range, suggesting broad agreement regarding the film's quality.

### 💯 Perfect Ratings Dominate

Nearly one out of every two reviewers awarded a perfect 10/10 score, reflecting unusually high audience satisfaction.

### 📉 Highly Left-Skewed Distribution

A skewness value of -2.199 reveals that ratings cluster heavily near the upper boundary of the scale, while low ratings form only a small minority.

### ✍️ Review Length Reveals Viewer Behavior

Moderately rated reviews tend to be longer and more analytical, whereas extremely positive or negative reviews are generally shorter and more emotionally driven.

## 📌 Conclusion

The analysis confirms that ***3 Idiots*** received **strong, widespread, and consistent audience approval**. High central tendency measures, a strong concentration of ratings within the 7–10 range, and significant negative skewness collectively indicate that positive sentiment was shared across the audience rather than being driven by a small group of reviewers.

This study demonstrates how descriptive statistical techniques can transform large collections of audience reviews into clear, objective insights about public perception and engagement.

**Beyond evaluating a single film, this study demonstrates how descriptive statistical methods can be used to convert large-scale audience feedback into objective, interpretable insights regarding public perception and engagement.**
